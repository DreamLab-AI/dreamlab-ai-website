Keybase → Nostr migration DreamLab community relay

Runs entirely on this machine. Paper key and admin key are kept in memory only. Generated member keys are written to work/keys.json (gitignored) and the CSV export.

1Connect to Keybase

If your desktop Keybase client is already logged in, no paper key is needed.
checking…

2Select channels & members

Pick the channels to migrate and the forum zone each lands in. Tick members to generate Nostr identities for them; their Keybase username becomes their handle. Fill in real names (prefilled from Keybase where available). Cohorts gate which zones a member can write to — public/friends zones need friends, etc.
CSV contains nsec private keys — share each row only with its owner, then delete.

3Export messages from Keybase

Pages through full history of every selected channel (300 msgs/page) into work/archive/. Re-running refreshes the archive.

  


  
  

    
4Build conversion plan (dry run)

    
Applies Keybase edits/deletes, resolves reply threads, maps senders to the
      generated identities, and reports what will be published. Nothing is written yet.

    

      

      

    

    

    

    

  


  
  

    
5Publish to DreamLab

    
Whitelists members and creates channels via the relay's NIP-98 admin API, then
      bulk-writes signed events. Direct D1 inserts straight into the
      dreamlab-relay database via wrangler (needs
      CLOUDFLARE_API_TOKEN / CLOUDFLARE_ACCOUNT_ID
      in the server's environment, or a prior wrangler login) — minutes, not hours.
      Relay websocket goes through full validation at ~8 events/sec. Both are idempotent: re-runs skip
      what's already there.
